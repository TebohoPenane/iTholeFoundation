iThole ICT Foundation is a None Profit Organisation (NPC) established by young people (YOUTH) in the outskirts of the Southern Western Townships called SOWETO, in Zola North.

iThole Foundation seeks to expose young people to the world of
Computing, Coding & Robotics.
iThole ICT Foundations runs Computer Classes to expose young people(YOUTH).
Young black girls in particular.
To encourage, equip & strike an intrest in young people to acquire Computing & Programming Skills in this Digital Era (4IR)

Vision:
In a Digital World Computing is Vital

We are building & grooming a generation that is thriving in IT, Robotics, and AI. A people in our communities, girls in particular, who will develop an interest in computer studies & pursue the fields of IT & Computer Science.
This is all because we understand that Computing is a vital aspect of today’s digital world, & we use computers in every career & field of study.

Mission: Computational Tools

AI & the Information Age, Robotics & the IoT

We will journey young people on the basic use of computers using Microsoft Office, & integrating it with their core subjects in Teaching & Learning. With the information age we will use technology & readily available digital devices to help learners navigate the vast amounts of information found in the internet. Taking the widespread of information narrowing it down, using it wisely & informatively and protecting themselves from identity theft & cyber bullying, whilst creating a positive Digital Footprint. Introducing young girls to Coding, Robotics & the AI. Young girls will be creating a Digital Profile & an online Resume to showcase their Programming Skills. They will build automated system & learn how to Build & Control Robots using Algorithms through Programming Languages Introduce Learners to the Digital Market & Animation. To help expand their creativity & artistic idea through Technology & Digital Devices

Our Program:
Gender Ballance in Computing

With Code Club we are encouraging young women to study computing & to acquire programming skills & strike an interest in computing to increase the numbers of young women to study Computer Science & bridge the skills gap in the field of IT The Program will Pilot with the young people (eYouth). We will therefore expand to reach out to every young girl in our community & ultimately in the landscapes of Soweto & Gauteng at large. Using Learning Resources from the Raspberry Pi Foundation, {Code}club plans to develop the program for kids from 5 years to 18 years old, to learn how to code. We will subdivide our Kids according to Key Stages to accommodate those who have never touched a computer before. The Programme is focused at developing young ones & training them in the ways of Computational Studies & Technology. We will teach them a variety of things including Computers and using ICT for Coding and for Teaching & Learning.

Goals | Objectives

To restructure & take lead in upskilling our young girls in Computing, Coding & ICT, preparing them for the age of technology in every area, Career or Field of Study. Our goal is to share Coding, Computing & Programming Skills to the under privileged young girls of SOWETO Townships. To spark an interest in young girls & see increasing numbers of young girls bridging the gap, taking up & pursuing Careers in the Fields of IT & Computer Science
